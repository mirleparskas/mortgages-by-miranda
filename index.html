FAQ

Straight answers before you book a call.

When should I get pre-approved?

Before serious house shopping. A pre-approval helps confirm budget, rate hold options, down payment requirements, and documents lenders may ask for.

Is the lowest rate always best?

No. Penalties, prepayment privileges, restrictions, portability, and approval conditions can matter as much as the rate.

What documents are usually needed?

Government ID, income documents, down payment history, credit and debt details, and property information are common starting points.

Can I refinance to consolidate debt?

Sometimes. The right answer depends on available equity, penalties, new payment structure, qualification, and total cost over time.
